You don't cross a threshold because you're ready. You cross it because staying where you are has become more painful than the unknown.

Most people who come to me aren't looking for answers. They already know the answer. What they need is someone to say it plainly — and then help them figure out what to do next.

## The Pattern You Already See

Here's the thing about patterns: you can usually name them. You know you keep choosing partners who can't show up. You know you abandon yourself the moment someone gets upset. You know you stay too long in situations that stopped serving you years ago.

The naming isn't the problem. The *stopping* is.

## Why Knowing Isn't Enough

Knowing a pattern exists doesn't break it. If it did, every self-help book you've read would have worked by now.

Patterns break when three things happen:

- You see the pattern clearly — without the story you've built around it
- You understand what it's protecting you from
- You take a different action, even when it feels wrong

That last part is where most people get stuck. Because the new action doesn't feel *better*. It feels uncomfortable, uncertain, sometimes terrifying. That's how you know it's the right one.

## The Invitation

If you're standing at a threshold right now — grief, fear, a cycle you can't seem to break — know this: you don't have to cross it alone. But you do have to be willing to cross it.

That willingness is everything.
